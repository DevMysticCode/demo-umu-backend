Auth flow

What changed.

No more navy header band
The original screens had a heavy navy lump at the top with the page title sitting in it. Replaced with a small back button + tiny umu logo on a white surface. The page now breathes from the top edge.
Warmer headlines
"Tell us who you are" → "Start with your home."
"Welcome back" → kept (works), but no longer in a navy band
"Forgot password?" → "No worries."
Each screen leads with reassurance, not extraction.
Mint reassurance card
The "What you get" card on the single-step Create Account screen lists three quiet benefits with teal checks. Sets up the value before asking for fields. Same chrome as the live app's mint-bordered cards.
Postcode incentive prompt
Below the postcode field: a small mint card explaining what they get for filling it in. Removes the "why is a property app asking for my postcode?" friction by showing the reward up-front.
Mobile reassurance line
Per your decision: kept mobile on the form as optional, with the line "We only text you about your Passport — never marketing." right under it. Tiny shield icon for trust signal.
Pill buttons, not slabs
All primary buttons are border-radius: 100px pills matching your live app's "View Expert Profile" / "Download PDF" pattern. Same calmer treatment as the Aisha CTA fix.
Marketing opt-in default OFF
GDPR-compliant. The checkbox starts unchecked and the language is honest: "Email me occasional updates. We won't spam you." Tap the row to toggle.
Logged-out toast — quieter
In your screenshot it's a big mint banner taking the top half of the page. Reduced to a small mint pill with a check icon. Good news that doesn't shout.
Two-step option (B)
Same content split across two screens. Step 1 = email + password (commitment). Step 2 = name + mobile + postcode (all optional, with a "Skip for now" button). Progress dots above the form so users know it's short.
Forgot password — two states
Entry screen ("No worries. Enter your email...") and sent-confirmation state with mint icon, "Check your email", and the user's email shown back. "Resend" link if it doesn't arrive.
Try this
Switch between A and B at the top. Tap any password show/hide eye. Tap the marketing checkbox to toggle. On Sign in, tap "Forgot password?" — it flows to the reset entry, then submit goes to the sent confirmation.
