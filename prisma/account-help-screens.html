Four screens · two flows · same family

Billing & Payment · the gilt plan card
The current plan gets the same deep-teal-and-gold treatment as the passport books and the buyer profile share card — the user sees their subscription as something premium they have access to, not a line item. Realistic Visa block built with pure CSS (chip, embossed numbers, italic VISA logo). Three months of invoices listed as PDF tiles with a small green "Paid" pill. The bottom action area has both an upgrade path (Compare plans) and a graceful exit (Cancel subscription, in coral so it's findable but not loud).
Settings · reassurance first, then organised
Opens with a security strength card — teal pulsing shield, "Your account is well protected", listing the three things that protect them (strong password, Face ID, 2FA). Sets the tone before any control list. Below, five clearly labelled sections: Account & security, Notifications, Appearance & language, Privacy & data, Legal. Each row has its own appropriate control — toggle, segmented selector for theme, value pill for current selection. The 2FA row gets a teal "On" pill in its title. Login activity surfaces "3 active devices" so users can audit their own access.
Learn & Ask AI · the breathing orb
A purple-teal-gold AI orb that gently breathes as the focal point — radial-gradient layers create a sense of energy without anthropomorphising. The headline mixes sans and italic serif: "Ask me anything about your property journey." Input field has a purple gradient send button and a microphone for voice. Four suggested prompts in tinted purple cards — covering the most common UK-property anxieties: EICR, conveyancing, stamp duty, surveys. Library section is reserved with two "Soon" cards (articles, video walk-throughs) so users know what's coming.
Help & Support · live, useful, contained
Live status card up top: green pulsing dot, "Support team is online · average reply under 4 hours" — turns a static page into something that feels responsive. Four contact options as a 2x2 grid: Ask AI is the warm purple-tinted primary (instant, 24/7), then Live chat (with hours), Email, and Phone. FAQ accordion uses editorial italic numerals (01, 02, 03…) for a touch of personality, with the first one expanded showing the answer with an embedded "Read more" link. System status at the bottom — "All systems operational" with a teal pulsing live pill — same trust-by-transparency thread.
Add Payment Method modal · live card preview
As the user types, a realistic card preview at the top fills in — cardholder name, last 4 digits, expiry. It's a small thing but it makes a tedious form feel like an interaction. Reuses the registered billing address with a one-tap override link. Trust pill at the bottom ("Secured by Stripe · we never store your full card number") is the kind of reassurance 35-65 users particularly appreciate when typing financial details.
AI Chat modal · a real conversation
Full-screen chat interface (not a small modal — chat needs the room). AI bubbles on the left with the small orb avatar; user bubbles on the right with the JP initials avatar. Key numbers in AI answers appear in bold teal. Typing dots animate while it's thinking. Bottom-docked input with paperclip for attachments and a purple gradient send button. Disclaimer line at the bottom: "UMU AI gives general guidance, not legal or financial advice" — protects the product without spoiling the friendly tone.
What ties this file to File 1
Same nav bar pattern. Same italic serif greeting + bold sans headline. Same hero stats line. Same section headings. Same card depth and corner radii. Same colour vocabulary (teal action, gold value, coral urgency, purple personal/social). Same modal pattern with handle, header, scrollable body, sticky footer. The user moving between any of these ten screens shouldn't feel like they've left the app — only the content changes.
